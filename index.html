Mission

Modern schooling too often separates knowledge from life. Eos reunites them. We create learning environments where science meets soil, mathematics meets water, and culture meets craft—so graduates are competent, resilient, and civically awake.

  • Ecological & civic literacy
  • Nutrition & food sovereignty
  • Skilled technical workforce pathways
  • Community resilience & well‑being

What’s different

Eos is a solution stack, not a slogan. We pair hands‑on curricula with teacher training, community partners, and a research‑grade evaluation—so districts can adopt with confidence.

Explore the model

The Eos model

1) Place‑based curricula

Math, science, and civics taught through real sites: gardens, kitchens, watersheds, maker‑spaces. Students learn systems thinking by managing living systems.

2) Food & health

School‑to‑farm pipelines and student‑run culinary labs improve nutrition, reduce waste, and connect families to fresh, local food.

3) Trades pathways

Early exposure to carpentry, fabrication, ag‑tech, and environmental monitoring—stackable micro‑credentials and apprenticeships with local employers.

4) Evaluation & policy

Matched‑control research tracks outcomes: attendance, academic gains, certifications, food metrics, teacher retention—producing a replication toolkit for districts.

Pilot sites (18–24 months)

We launch in three communities—urban, suburban, and rural—to prove replicability across contexts. Each site includes gardens, a learning kitchen, and a small fabrication lab.
